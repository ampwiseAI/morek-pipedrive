Profile settings

Click your avatar (top right) → Personal preferences → Profile.

✓

✓
Set your local timezone.
✓

Why a real photo? Your face appears in reports, activity logs, and team meetings. It's how teammates recognise you across hundreds of records.
Why your full name? Names show up in reports and audit trails. "Mart" is fine in chat. "Mart Tamm" is what the system needs.

Step 2 / 5

Outlook email sync

Connects your Outlook inbox to Pipedrive so emails auto-attach to People, Organisations, and Deals. The single biggest lever in this whole setup.

Click-by-click

1
Settings → Email sync.
2
Choose Microsoft 365 / Outlook / Exchange. Sign in with Microsoft (OAuth).
3
Sync direction: Two-way.
4
Sync past emails: set the maximum period you want (gets old client conversations in for context).
5
Email tracking: activate all three features.
6
Default visibility: Shared. Visible to teammates only when the email is linked to a Pipedrive contact.
7
Synced folders: all folders by default.

Watch out for these

!
In General settings, make sure auto-archive and auto-delete are OFF. You don't want Pipedrive deleting your inbox.
!
Disable automatic linking if your clients send emails titled "Inquiry" or "RE: inquiry". Otherwise threads link to the wrong deal.
i
Mark emails Private when they contain salary, contracts, legal, HR, or sensitive partner negotiations.
Need help with the signature? Copy from Outlook. Keep it short. Full name, role, phone with country code, company. Skip the giant logo, it triggers spam filters.

Step 3 / 5

Calendar sync

Settings → Calendar sync. Connects your Outlook calendar so meetings created either side appear on the other.

Click-by-click

1
Pick Microsoft 365 / Outlook / Exchange. Sign in (OAuth).
2
Choose the right calendar if you have several.
3
Direction: Two-way.
4
Outlook meetings appear in Pipedrive as "Other tasks". Do NOT pick "Meeting" as default.
5
Make sure Outlook tasks also show up in your Pipedrive activity list.
6
At the bottom of the page: "Sync linked contact and deal details" = Do not add.
Pro tip: Always create activities (calls, meetings, follow-ups) inside Pipedrive. They flow into Outlook automatically. The reverse is messier.
Private events: either set them visible only to you or don't sync them at all. Birthday plans don't need to be in your sales CRM.
If you sync deal details into calendar events by mistake, your client meetings start showing private deal values to whoever's looking over your shoulder.

Client classes

A, B, C client classes

Every organisation gets a class. The class sets the minimum number of activities per year required to keep the account healthy. Below your minimum and the account starts drifting.

A
Top tier

120k+ revenue

12+ activities/year minimum

Strategic accounts. Monthly contact, quarterly visits, lunch invites, event invites, joint planning.

B
Mid tier

30k - 120k revenue

6 - 8 activities/year minimum

Solid accounts with growth potential. Bi-monthly check-in, twice-yearly visit, campaign emails.

C
Long tail

Under 30k revenue

4 activities/year minimum

Transactional. Quarterly touchpoint, group emails, automated reactivation when needed.

The #1 reason accounts leave: low volume of right actions from the account manager. Especially when competitors are active. Your minimums protect against this.
Account roadmap

The account roadmap

Imagine you're the client. Your current supplier just takes orders. Then a competitor calls, visits, talks growth, takes you to lunch. Sooner or later, you switch.
That's the gap your roadmap closes.

Right actions, on rotation

✓
Visits: face-to-face, on their site, at least quarterly for A clients.
✓
Calls: real conversations, not just order confirmations.
✓
Lunches and events: yes, this counts. Build the relationship.
✓
Growth conversations: what's their plan for next year, where can Morek help?
✓
New product intros: Q-LINE, Phoenix EVC, Moflex. They want to hear about these.

Wrong actions, alone

×
Just emails about daily orders
×
No visit in 6+ months
×
Reacting only when they ask
×
Same conversation every time
Roles change. Goals shift. You won't catch any of that through purchase orders. Activities catch it. Activities keep the account.

Pro tips

Don't make these mistakes

The little things that quietly ruin your reporting and forecasting.

×
Don't leave 0 € as the value. Pipeline metrics depend on it. Put an estimate even if you're not sure.
×
Don't skip the label. No label = no filtering = no reporting on tenders, frameworks, etc.
×
Don't create a deal without a next activity. A deal without an activity is a deal you'll forget.
×
Don't skip the search step. Duplicates kill reporting and confuse teammates.
✓
Always pick a Deal origin. Cold call, event, referral. Tells the team where pipeline comes from.
✓
Use Important Info for static context (who introduced you, why this matters). Use Notes/Activities for ongoing updates.
✓
Schedule the next activity immediately after saving. Even just "Call Friday 10:00".
✓
Move the deal to the right stage right away. Lead, Qualify, Present. Don't leave new deals stuck in Lead.

Pick the right one

Three pipelines

Each one has its own stages because the work is different. Pick wrong and your deal sits in the wrong column forever.

1. New business

Land new wholesalers, contractors, end clients.

Stages: Lead · Qualify · Meet · 1st offer · Negotiate · Final offer · Waiting · Onboarding · First order won

Example: Get a new PV installer to stock Q-LINE.

2. Existing business

Grow what you already have.

Stages: Map needs · Meet · 1st offer · Negotiate · Final offer · Confirmation

Example: Add Phoenix EVC to a current OTL customer.

3. Designers

Architects who specify Morek in projects.

Stages: Prospect · Reach · Map needs · Tools shared · First project prescribed

Example: Get Moflex busbars specced into a high-rise build.

A specced-in Moflex busbar on a 20-floor build is a 6-figure framework deal. One designer relationship can pay your bonus for the year.
